Chronicles of Cinder

Uncover the forgotten tales etched in ash and ember

Historical Figure

The Ashen King

Once a noble ruler who sought immortality through dark rituals. His reign ended in catastrophe when the ritual consumed his kingdom.

Race

Children of Ember

Born from the ashes of the Great Burning, these beings possess an innate connection to flame and can withstand extreme heat.

Deity

Sorath, the Scorched God

The deity of rebirth through destruction. Sorath teaches that only through fire can true transformation occur.

Major Event

The Great Burning

An apocalyptic event that reshaped the world. The sky turned black with ash for a century as civilizations crumbled.

Creature

Cinder Wyrms

Serpentine beasts that burrow through volcanic rock. Their scales glow with internal heat, and their breath melts steel.

Location

The Scorched Citadel

An ancient fortress built within an active volcano. Its obsidian walls serve as sanctuary for those who embrace the flame.

Race

The Ashborn

Pale-skinned humanoids who thrive in ash-choked wastelands, adapted to breathe toxic air and sense heat through smoke.

Historical Figure

Lady Emberlyn

A pyromancer of unparalleled skill whose sacrifice created the Eternal Pyre, a flame burning for 500 years without fuel.

The Age of Cinder

Chronicle the epochs that shaped our world

Year 47 of Sol

The First Flame Awakens

Deep beneath the mountains, an ancient fire stirs. Miners report hearing whispers in the depths, speaking in a language lost to time. The temperature rises with each passing day. Within weeks, the volcano erupts with unprecedented fury, birthing the Children of Ember and marking the beginning of a new age. The world would never be the same.

Year 52 of Sol

Rise of the Ashen King

King Malachar discovers an ancient tome that promises eternal life through mastery of flame. Driven by fear of mortality, he begins conducting dark rituals in secret. His advisors notice his changing demeanor—obsessive, withdrawn, speaking of "transcending the flesh." The kingdom prospers under his rule, unaware of the doom he is bringing upon them all.

Year 98 of Sol

The Great Burning

The Ashen King completes his ritual. What follows is catastrophic. A pillar of black flame erupts from the castle, spreading across the land like wildfire. Entire cities turn to ash in moments. The sky darkens with soot that will not clear for a hundred years. Survivors speak of seeing shadows in the smoke—the souls of the consumed. This marks the darkest period in recorded history.

Year 105 of Sol

Foundation of the Scorched Citadel

Amid the chaos, a group of flame-wielders establishes a sanctuary within an active volcano. They recognize that fire, while destructive, can also protect. The Citadel becomes a beacon for refugees who can withstand the heat. Its founders develop techniques to channel volcanic energy into protective wards, creating the only truly safe haven in the Age of Ash.

Year 149 of Sol

Lady Emberlyn's Sacrifice

Ice elementals pour through a rift in the north, threatening to extinguish all flame and freeze the world. Lady Emberlyn, the realm's most powerful pyromancer, leads a desperate defense. In the final battle, she channels all her life force into a single spell—the Eternal Pyre. The flame banishes the elementals and seals the rift, but costs her everything. The pyre burns still, a monument to her sacrifice.

Year 157 of Sol

The Ash Clears

For the first time in nearly sixty years, survivors glimpse the sun. The perpetual ashfall finally subsides, settling into a thick layer that blankets the land. New plant life begins to emerge—strange flora adapted to the ash-rich soil. The Ashborn race is discovered living in the ruins, having evolved to thrive in the toxic environment. Hope returns, cautious but genuine.

Year 198 of Sol

The New Covenant

The major races convene at the Scorched Citadel to forge an alliance. The Children of Ember, Ashborn, and surviving human kingdoms agree to share knowledge and resources. Flame magic is regulated through a council to prevent another catastrophe. Trade routes are established through the ash wastes. Civilization begins to rebuild, forever changed by the trials of fire.

Year 203 of Sol

Discovery of the Cinder Wyrms

Explorers venturing into volcanic regions encounter massive serpentine creatures—Cinder Wyrms. These beings seem to have existed dormant during the Great Burning, awakening only now. Initial conflicts prove costly, but eventually a fragile understanding is reached. The wyrms are intelligent and ancient, possessing knowledge of the world before the flames. Their testimony rewrites history.
