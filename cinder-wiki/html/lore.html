The Ages of Itero

Chronicle the epochs that shaped our world

0-400 AD

The First Age Begins

The first recorded age of Itero. Humans, Gorgons and Elves have established kingdoms prior to the start of this documented age whereas races such as the Drow and Dark Elves whilst present in this moment merely live like tribes in their respective environments. (Comparable to the early bronze age living)

300 AD

Ascension of the Nightmare Child

The Nightmare Child is born (Around year 300), within his mortal lifetime ascends to godhood, after which he slays the Vatision Pantheon (7 Gods) and decimates Elysium. The Nightmare child later joins the Darkmoon pantheon with his Lich brethren.

401-446 AD

The Great Dying

The Great Dying (aka Morthrax/ Thrax) claims an estimated four million lives from 401AD to 446AD across all continents, slowly spreading from one corner to another. The main races afflicted were Humans, Dwarves and other sub-humans. Many of which as either a direct or indirect result would go extinct. This event made Humans a rarer species across the continent of Helheim where it was most prevalent (40-60% of all deaths). The disease would seemingly dip for multiple years before re emerging. No cases of the disease have been authenticated after 446AD. The Elves degrade their unspoken alliance with the humans, forbidding their kind from entering their forests as to prevent the spread of Morthrax to the Elven people. As a result only 1% of all deaths attributed to the disease are of Elven Descent, many of which are elves who lived in human settlements- suggesting that their kind has some form of natural resistance to the disease in the first place.

402 - 425 AD

Lich Migration to Helheim

The Lich migrate to Helheim from Elysium, in the wake of ever decreasing human populations. This offered them an immense number of soldiers for their wars and feuds.

500 AD

Djinn Independence and Lycan Mutation

The Djinns declare themselves independent in new land and station a new kingdom where they can prosper atop of the old eastern human capital. The Lycanthropy curse mutates and results in the Lycan race, a species of people who are neither extreme (human or beast- but in the middle). The Abyss is publicly documented for the first time, shortly followed by massive reports of missing people and the appearance of “Cthulites”, a lesser and failed attempt at what would later become the Eldritch. The Drow migrate to Helheim and form a new Kingdom. The Dwarves declare independence from the Humans and form their own Kingdom whilst keeping friendly relations with the previous faction.

600 AD

Lich Dominance in Helheim

The Liches begin posing a dominance over Helheim, utilising an immense number of raised undead to pressure other races and taking advantage of the now unstable balance across the realm- all under the iron clad rule of the scourge emperor Ozymandias. The war against the Elves begins.

700 AD

Rise of Alchemy

The school of Alchemy rapidly grows and it proves to be more efficient than magic for curing certain ailments. It also allowed businesses to sell a product rather than a service which was not only far more time efficient but lucrative.

901 AD

Djinn Conquest

Selion Soup marries into Djinn royalty, the ceremony after which he declares his plans for war. The Lich King Ozymandias is killed here and the Liches are subjugated, alongside the Gorgons. This marks the day when the Djinn took control over Helheim. Helheim is never the same after this.

910 AD

Lycan Independence

Lycans declare themselves independent, growing their kingdoms across old Gorgon territory. The Orcs migrate from the dark forests setting up their new settlements in abandoned battlefields.

913 AD

Djinn Colonisation

The Djinn begin sending troops and civilians to colonies in order to strengthen their nation internationally. Unintentionally weakening their home front.

1100 AD

Lich Independence

The Liches declare themselves independent from the Djinn. Skian becomes the new king of the Liches, to later be known as “The Overlord”.

1103 AD

Overlord's Conquest

Skian (The Overlord) proves his leadership abilities swiftly to his ever anxious numbers; taking notice of the Djinns withering forces strikes swift and hard, conquering over 80% of all Djinn owned in land provinces, forcing much of their numbers into labour. This act is committed both out of revenge for the previous years, which dwell heavily on the mind of many a lich, but also as an act of enrichment. Much of their previous strength, resources and knowledge had been lost to war if not to the Djinns themselves.

1125 AD

Elf Perish

The elf race devastated by the long Lich wars and deprived of resources by the Djinns practically perished. Many elves fled to other realms over the prior years following the rise of the Djinn. The Lycans bordering the Elf territory quickly begin claiming now deserted towns and regions. Alongside any treasures left behind in the devastation- angering the Lich who felt that these rewards were rightfully theirs The Dwarves growing in numbers but still ever weak request assistance from the Lich in return the dwarves would teach about their rune craft, enchanting and other fascinating revelations. The Cthulites declare themselves a growing major faction. The Orcs rip themselves apart with a bloody religious war, which quickly wipes almost all Orcs from Helheim. As a result the Dwarves become a minor nation.

1126 AD

Lycan War

Lycans under the leadership of Oedipus Chillbrand refuse to cooperate with the Liches shockingly peaceful demands (at least when compared to past behaviour), refusing to relinquish elven territory and treasure to the Lich. Angered and forced into a corner, the Liches prepare for a war. The Dwarves agree to assist the Liches in the war. The Cthulites seeking knowledge and better relations with major factions agree to lend aid in the war. The Lycans are later eradicated in this war, which unified the many races of the realm. But much of the national treasures of the Elves was hidden such that the Liches may never find it. A bounty, perhaps with the largest reward ever seen- is placed on the head of a sly elf who had been pulling the strings from the shadows, encouraging the Lycan behaviour and trying to undermine the leadership of many races including his own in order to obtain power, by the name of Kan Bowrain. Many races wish to torture him, others enslave him.

1240 AD

Djinn Release

The Djinn are released from the Liches, Skian no longer feeling anger towards their previous transgressions and realising he doesn’t wish their culture or people to continue mingling within his own, perhaps in concern of the dissent. This greatly disappoints the Cthulites who greatly desired the djinn as slaves and also unbeknownst to the other factions as offerings to the still mostly unknown Abyss.

1290 AD

Djinn Migration

The Djinn finished a mass (almost entire) migration to Exul and other external colonies outside of Helheim. Chased out by the now once more powerful Lich race amongst others.

1295 AD

Human Reconstruction

The Human Races including the Dwarves begin constructing massive towns and capitals across Helheim. Becoming one of the next major powers. The Dwarves hold true to their original alliances with the Liches. As a result the Liches at this stage are partially industrialised with assistance from their new allies. This marks the point in which the humans have truly bounced back from the devastation of the Great Dying.

1301 AD

Age of Man

The Humans set up capitals on every continent and enforce their presence as the new and sole major power globally, marking the age of man. Led by innovations in sorcery, technology and politics.

1373 AD

Wars Declared on Humans

War on multiple fronts is declared upon the Humans, their prosperity has captured the attention of a wide range of hungering races.

1388 AD

Human Empire Collapse Imminent

The collapse of the human empire is seemingly imminent as wars have left entire generations parentless- the next wave of troops still in essence cradle bound raised entirely by the church- many of which follow the Nine.

1394 AD

Human Surrender

All major human populations, including major cities, trade hubs and export hubs are given over to their attackers. The war wasn’t not concluded instead it simply seems to go quiet.

1396 AD

New Monarch

The last monarch of the current royal family perishes. The crown falls to another prestigious family- the Medleys. The King is now Titus Medley. He has two sons and one daughter.

1399 AD

Crusader Retaliation

The next generation now grown and entwined with the religious belief they were raised with and retaliated to massive success. The enemies seemingly not expecting resistance- especially not by a well coordinated force. Though the humans were outmatched and outnumbered- each man was willing to die so long as they had killed more soldiers then they were worth. In a few short months the enemy had retreated and man had reclaimed their territory. Though they were now referred to as the Crusaders.

1401-1735 AD

Age of Dawn

The Age of Dawn follows the rise of the Crusaders and the dominance of the Dawn Pantheon, spreading light and order across the realms.

1736 AD Onwards

The Shattered Age

Akhaten steals a cinder of creation and shatters the world, banishing gods to the void and hiding in Hell.

Prime Evil Pantheon

Nephra

Lord of the Vile

Nephra, Lord of the Vile, is most renowned for being the mother of the goddesses Vashara and Lathandra. Deeply disgusted by her offspring, Nephra loathed their perceived weakness and petty ambitions. Unlike herself, they desired to nurture and create new beings, finding beauty in what she considered vile. When Nephra discovered that Vashara had secretly conceived a child, she acted as the goddess of the vile would: she defiled and slaughtered, even attempting to eradicate her own progeny. Upon learning of this atrocity, Vashara tore the spine from her mother's body, fashioning it into a spear to pierce Nephra's still-beating heart. Thus ended the reign of the prime evil god, Nephra. Slain by Vashara (Darkmoon).

Azazel

Lord of Light

Azazel, the Lord of Light, was verily a prime evil deity of malevolence, and remains among the most reviled to this very day. Forsooth, harboured a profound abhorrence for humanity, and his loathing manifested through a relentless butchery of mankind. Villages and hamlets were reduced to ruin and cinders under his fervent wrath. Thus, when mortals clawed their way from their graves, Azazel's ire knew no bounds. The death of Raan had ushered in a scourge of undeath, to his utter dismay. In his fury, he cast a dire curse upon all undead, decreeing that the light itself would inexorably render them to ashes. But in his lifetime, Azazel beheld the adaptability of the human races, witnessing even the undead find ways to morph his curse. Frightened by the unrelenting progress and evolution of man, and their indomitable ingenuity, he withdrew. Yet unbeknownst to him, new challengers plotted their revenge, inspired by the fall of Raan. Kythos, a lesser god at this juncture, seized this golden opportunity to strike. It is said that Kythos stormed the castle of Averous with more than a hundred frightful lightning drakes- beings of his design, who swiftly tore asunder any who dared impede their path. Ultimately, Kythos impaled Azazel upon his throne with his mighty spear, absorbing the Lord of Light's power and merging it with his own to forge something new, something truly blessed and thus the sun rose from its meagre set. Slain by Kythos (Dawn).

The Hollow Goddess Rosaria

Goddess of Beauty

Rosaria was said to be as petite and delicate as a rose petal, the most exquisitely endowed of all the gods, a distinction only fitting for the one and only goddess of beauty. Her splendour was so unparalleled that she became wholly obsessed with all things beautiful. Her followers would pay tribute with vast fields of flowers and gorgeous paintings in her honour. Yet, her obsession led her down a dark path. She began to wonder how beautiful the Primordial One must be, for surely the creator of all beauty must be the pinnacle of such a pedestal. Such was her conviction. It swiftly became her singular goal to find the Primordial One and lay her eyes upon it, just as the masses travelled to behold her own radiance. And it would seem in time she did... No one knows how or where- but whatever she beheld must have been utterly forbidden, for her mind was lost at a mere glance. Now she is the Hollow Goddess, who raves with a quiet madness, her every function is left shuffled. Perhaps it was the knowledge alone that sent her spiralling, but her subtle ravings carry an aura of fear and despair that makes one think otherwise…

The Pale Moon Alana

Goddess of the Forlorn

Alana of the Pale Moon, often referred to as simply the “Palemoon” a nickname she acquired as a consequence of kinship to the darkmoon- but too a recognition that she does not follow their dark tradition, in her wake follows a sombre dim but comfortable light. The Palemoon is a goddess of the Forlorn. She who cradles the forgotten and disregarded who must only pledge themselves away from other beings. All these elements have resulted in her being a less than popular idol within her own pantheon, but yet she is a much beloved goddess to those who are patron to her teachings. It is believed that Alana raised the beast gods Kars and Tars, Gods of Predators and Prey from a young age and it was she who helped them create the Lycanthrope blessing. A method in which the measly and lost could achieve their place in beasthood. For this reason it is the moon that grants the Lycan their power and activates their blessing and thus the Lycanthropes above all else worship the Palemoon with the greatest intent and loyalty.
